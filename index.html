Ludo is a classic board game that can be played by 2 to 4 players.

The game involves moving your pieces around the board based on the roll of a die. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to play Ludo:

Objective:

The main objective of Ludo is to move all your four pieces from the starting area to your home column in the center of the board.

Setup:

1. Place the Ludo board in the center of the playing area.
2. Each player chooses a set of four pieces of the same color and places them in the starting area of the corresponding color.

Gameplay:

1. Players take turns in a clockwise order class.
2. On your turn, roll a six-sided die. Move one of your pieces forward the number of spaces indicated by the die.
- If you roll a 6, you get an extra turn class.
- If you cannot move any piece based on the number rolled, your turn passes to the next player.
3. Pieces move around the board following the path of their respective color. Pieces must complete a circuit of the board before entering the home column.

Capturing:

- If your piece lands on an opponent's piece, you capture that piece, and the opponent's piece goes back to its starting area.
- You cannot capture pieces in your own home column.

Entering the Home Column:

- Once a piece completes a circuit, it can enter its home column. To do this, you need to roll the exact number needed to reach the starting point of your home column.

Winning:

- The first player to move all four pieces into their home column wins the game.

Additional Rules:

1. If you roll a 6, you get another turn. If you roll three 6s in a row, your turn is skipped, and you must return one of your pieces to the starting area.
2. Players can strategically choose which piece to move based on the dice roll and the current board situation.
